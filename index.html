accommodations + travel

Acommodations

The wedding and related festivities will take place in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, New York. There are many hotels, restaurants, and shops in the area, and it's easy to get to many other parts of New York City from Williamsburg.

We've reserved a block of hotel rooms at the New York Marriott at the Brooklyn Bridge, about 20 minutes by cab or Lyft from Brooklyn Winery. There are many other hotels in the vicinity of the venue, as well as in nearby Manhattan. Airbnb is also a popular option for those who prefer it. We suggest booking your lodging soon, as the holidays are a popular time to visit the city!

Getting Here

The only parking available at Brooklyn Winery is street parking, so we recommend arriving to the venue by subway, cab, or Lyft.

Address

Brooklyn Winery, 213 North 8th Street Brooklyn, NY 11211 (between Driggs Ave. & Roebling St.)

By Subway

L Train

Take the L train to the Bedford Ave. stop (first stop in Brooklyn when coming from Manhattan). Exit at either Bedford or Driggs and walk north to North 8th St. Walk east on North 8th St. and the winery will be on the north side of the street between Driggs Ave. and Roebling St.

G Train

Take the G Train to the Metropolitan Ave. stop. Exit subway, walk north on Union Ave. toward Conselyea St., and go under the Brooklyn Queens Expressway (BQE). North. 8th St. will be in front of you and a bit to the left of Union Ave.; follow N. 8th St and walk 3.5 blocks. The winery will be on the north side of the street between Driggs Ave. and Roebling St.

By Car

From Manhattan taking Williamsburg Bridge

From the Williamsburg Bridge take the exit toward I-278 W/Brooklyn Queens Expressway. Turn left onto Havemeyer St. and then a slight left onto South 4th St. Turn right onto Roebling St. and take for .5 miles. Take a left onto North 8th St. The winery will be on the north side of the street between Driggs Ave. and Roebling St.

Brooklyn Queens Expressway (I-278) Going East

Take exit 32 toward Metropolitan Ave/Williamsburg Bridge/Manhattan and merge onto Rodney St. Continue onto Meeker Ave. (going north) and take a left at Union Ave. Go under the BQE and take the first left onto Meeker Ave. (going south), the take the first right onto North 8th St. The winery will be on the north side of the street between Driggs Ave. and Roebling St.

Brooklyn Queens Expressway (I-278) Going West

Take exit 32B toward Metropolitan Ave. and merge onto Meeker Ave. Take the first right onto North 8th St. The winery will be on the north side of the street between Driggs Ave. and Roebling St.

the story of seph + sam

Sam "comps" Harvard's rock radio department, Record Hospital. Seph and Parker (the best man) are in charge of deciding if she makes it on. Despite Sam missing a session to go to a Katy Perry concert, they deem her worthy and let her join.

Spring, 2009

Seph and Sam go to a Japandroids show together, after all their friends bail, losing their voices to "Young Hearts Spark Fire."

Fall, 2009

Both live in Cambridge for the summer. They go to many more shows, and Seph invites himself over to watch the World Cup on Sam's tv. Sam could care less about soccer but the courtship is underway.

Summer, 2010

Despite having tickets to a Pavement reunion show in New York, Sam bails on the trip and goes to a party with Seph at the Advocate instead. They dance themselves clean.

Fall, 2010

They graduate, and forego worrying about the lack of job prospects to spend the summer traveling in Europe. They chill in Iceland, stay with friends in Cambridge and Paris, and work a little too long at a Spanish a horse farm run by loutish Brits.

Summer, 2011

They decide to try their luck in San Francisco. Sam packs just a suitcase. A few months later, Seph gets his driving permit, and they take turns driving her car from Cape Cod to SF by way of Chicago, St. Louis, the Grand Canyon, and San Diego.

Fall, 2011

Seph and Sam move in to a beautiful apartment with their friends Will and Alison. On a whim, they drive to Marin, buy four baby chicks, and build them a coop.

Spring, 2013

They travel around South America, and end up in Brazil at the World Cup. Sam starts to come around on the whole soccer thing.

Summer, 2014

After three and a half years, two apartments, countless camping trips, four bikes, and several hairstyle changes, Seph and Sam move to Brooklyn, to the delight of both their families.

Spring, 2015

Seph and Sam learn what a co-op apartment is, and buy one. It is an eminently dog friendly building, and Ruby soon joins the family.

Spring, 2016

On a rainy Sunday morning in October, Seph wakes up Sam (a bit on the early side, if she's being honest) and asks her to marry him. Of course, she says yes.

Fall, 2016
